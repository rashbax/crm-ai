Route: /approvals

Purpose
This is the founder decision queue. It is used when routine managers want to make a change that crosses a business threshold and must be reviewed before execution.

Approval model
- Requests can cover price changes, promotions, ad budgets, stock scaling, ownership changes, or other exceptional actions.
- Each request stores entity type, approval type, priority, requester, reason, current value, new value, optional business impact, optional marketplace and SKU, and optional expiry date.

Main features
- Status cards for Pending, Approved, and Rejected requests.
- Signal cards and banners for critical requests, stale requests with no answer for more than 24 hours, and expired requests.
- Search and priority filtering.
- Table that clearly shows the proposed change, business impact, requester, status, decision comment, and deadline.

Decision workflow
- New request modal for manual approval submission.
- Decision modal for founder approval or rejection.
- Decision comments are mandatory, which forces documented reasoning.
- Rejected requests can be resubmitted with an updated reason or updated new value.

Business value
- Keeps major commercial decisions under control without slowing down routine work.
- Creates a clean audit trail for who requested a risky change, what exactly was changing, and why leadership approved or rejected it.
- Reduces the chance of silent margin damage or uncontrolled operational changes.
